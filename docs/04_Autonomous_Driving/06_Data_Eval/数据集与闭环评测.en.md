## 1. Why Data and Evaluation Are the Lifeblood

Autonomous driving is a **long-tail-dominated** problem: 99% of miles are uneventful, and safety is decided by the rare 1% (a child darting out, construction reroutes, oddly shaped obstacles). So "what data you train on" and "what ruler you measure with" often decide success more than the model architecture. This note splits data and evaluation into three layers: **perception datasets** (with sensors and annotations), **prediction/planning benchmarks** (with scenarios and futures), and **simulation & closed-loop evaluation** (where the policy actually drives). The bottom line up front: **open-loop offline metrics systematically overestimate real performance; closed-loop is the trustworthy ruler** (mechanism in [Covariate Shift & the Closed-Loop Gap](../05_Learning_E2E/协变量偏移与闭环鸿沟.en.md)).

---

## 2. Perception Datasets

Perception datasets provide synchronized multi-sensor raw data with 3D annotations for training/evaluating detection, segmentation, and tracking.

| Dataset | Year | Sensors | Scale | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| KITTI | 2012 | stereo camera + 64-line LiDAR | ~15k annotated frames | foundational AD perception benchmark |
| nuScenes | 2020 | 6 cameras + 32-line LiDAR + 5 radars | 1000 clips ×20s, full 360° | first full-sensor surround, with map & attribute labels |
| Waymo Open | 2020 | 5 cameras + 5 LiDARs (mid/short range) | 1150 clips, dense labels | large scale, precise labels, long-range LiDAR |
| Argoverse 2 | 2021 | surround cameras + dual LiDAR | 1000 clips + large-scale maps | emphasis on HD maps and motion forecasting |
| ONCE | 2021 | camera + LiDAR | 1M frames (mostly unlabeled) | large-scale data for semi-/self-supervision |

**Key dimensions**: sensor configuration (determines usable modalities), annotation density and classes, presence of HD maps, geographic/weather diversity. Choosing a dataset is essentially trading off "closeness to your deployment ODD" against "annotation quality/scale."

## 3. Prediction and Planning Benchmarks

- **Motion forecasting benchmarks**: Argoverse / Argoverse 2 Motion Forecasting, Waymo Motion, nuScenes prediction. Given history + HD map, predict multi-agent future trajectories (see [Motion Prediction](../03_Prediction_Planning/运动预测.en.md)). Metrics: minADE/minFDE/Miss Rate (see §5).
- **Planning benchmarks**: **nuPlan** is the first large-scale **real-data closed-loop** planning benchmark — it does not just compare trajectories but lets the planner **actually drive** the vehicle, distinguishing two modes:
  - **non-reactive**: background traffic replayed from log, not reacting to ego;
  - **reactive**: background traffic reacts to ego via models like IDM, closer to real interaction.
  nuPlan marks the shift of planning evaluation from "open-loop answer-checking" to "closed-loop consequence."

---

## 4. Simulation and Closed-Loop Evaluation

Real road testing is expensive and dangerous; simulation is the key infrastructure for scalable closed-loop evaluation and training.

- **CARLA**: open-source urban driving simulator with sensor simulation, traffic flow, and weather. Its **Leaderboard** runs closed-loop on preset routes, scoring **driving score = route completion × infraction penalty** (red-light running, collision, line-crossing, timeout each deduct). It is the de facto closed-loop arena for end-to-end methods (TransFuser, Roach, etc.).
- **Closed-loop vs open-loop**: closed-loop has the policy self-drive, exposing [covariate shift](../05_Learning_E2E/协变量偏移与闭环鸿沟.en.md) and error accumulation; open-loop only checks answers on a fixed expert trajectory, never leaving the distribution.
- **Reactive vs non-reactive background traffic**: if background cars do not react to ego (non-reactive), the policy can "bully" its way through with an inflated score; reactive traffic exposes real interaction.

## 5. Evaluation Metrics

| Layer | Metric | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Perception | mAP / NDS (nuScenes) | detection mean average precision / combined detection score |
| Prediction | minADE / minFDE | average/final displacement error of the best of K candidate trajectories |
| Prediction | Miss Rate | fraction whose best candidate endpoint exceeds a threshold |
| Planning (open-loop) | L2 displacement error | point-wise distance between predicted and expert trajectory |
| Planning/driving (closed-loop) | success / collision / violation / takeover | safety and completion under the policy actually driving |

**Key warning**: low open-loop L2 ≠ closed-loop safety. Codevilla et al. (2018) found empirically that open-loop metrics correlate only weakly — even negatively — with closed-loop driving performance; the root cause is that open-loop locks the model onto the expert distribution and cannot measure behavior after leaving it (see [Covariate Shift & the Closed-Loop Gap](../05_Learning_E2E/协变量偏移与闭环鸿沟.en.md) §3).

## 6. Data Engine and the Long Tail

The core competitiveness of a production system is the **data engine**:

- **Shadow mode**: run a new model in the background of production cars, log scenarios where it disagrees with the human driver / live model, and reflow them for targeted training;
- **Hard-example mining**: active retrieval of long-tail scenarios — takeovers/disengagements, near-collisions, oddly shaped obstacles;
- **Synthetic data**: simulation and generative world models (GAIA-1, etc.) synthesize rare dangerous scenarios to fill the tail that real data cannot capture (see [End-to-End Driving](../05_Learning_E2E/端到端驾驶.en.md)).

A data engine essentially keeps adding the high-risk tail of the distribution $d_\pi$ the policy actually encounters into the training set — the industrialized way of fighting covariate shift.

## Method Comparison

| Evaluation method | Cost | Exposes covariate shift | Stage | Representative |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Open-loop offline (L2/mAP/minADE) | low | no | module/component iteration | nuScenes/Argoverse leaderboards |
| Closed-loop simulation (driving) | medium | yes | system-level integration | CARLA Leaderboard, nuPlan |
| Real road testing | high | yes | final pre-deployment validation | takeover rate / MPI |

---

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Concluding from open-loop L2/mAP alone**: gets systematically misled by covariate shift; closed-loop validation is mandatory before deployment.
2. **Closed-loop with non-reactive background traffic**: non-reacting background cars inflate scores; use reactive traffic (e.g., nuPlan's reactive track).
3. **Dataset mismatched with deployment ODD**: training on sunny-city data but deploying to rainy-night suburbs is a direct source of covariate shift.
4. **Ignoring the long tail**: chasing metrics on average miles under-covers the 1% rare scenarios that decide safety.
5. **Inconsistent metric definitions**: different K for minADE, time horizons for L2, and violation weights distort cross-paper comparison.

---

## Frontier Trends

1. **Hybrid real-sim closed loop**: after nuPlan/CARLA, combine real-log replay with simulated reactive traffic to approach the true distribution.
2. **Generative world models for the long tail**: synthesize rare dangerous scenarios for closed-loop training and evaluation.
3. **Closed-loop becomes first-class**: evaluation focus shifts from open-loop leaderboards to closed-loop success/safety scores.
4. **Large-scale unlabeled + self-supervision**: ONCE-style massive unlabeled data drives self-supervised pretraining.
